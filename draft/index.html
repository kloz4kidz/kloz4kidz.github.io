About Kloz 4 Kidz

We are proud to serve Snohomish County families in a friendly, positive, affirming atmosphere.

Founded by educators with a belief that all schoolchildren deserve to feel good about their wardrobe, we are dedicated to providing kids from any family, regardless of income, a traditional school clothes shopping experience.

Appointments

If you are in need of free clothing for your school-aged Snohomish County child or children, we are here to help. We have reopened for clothing pick-ups, but we are still unable to provide indoor shopping or a changing room space. Appointments will be held outdoors for the health and safety of our clients and volunteers.

Please note that jeans are not available at this time due to lack of fitting room availability.

Directions for our current appointment process are below. Before you begin, please have your child's clothing sizes available.

  1. Click the 'Book Now' button below and select an appointment time. Please choose a time you and your child will be available to come to Marysville to pick up and fit your child's clothing.
  2. Each child will receive 3 shirts, 2 sweatshirts, 1 pair of sweatpants, leggings (girls only), a pair of shorts, 3 pairs of socks, 3 pairs of underwear, and one pair of shoes. Please note that at this time, jeans are not available.
  3. Fill out the appointment form. Be sure to provide us with information about each child you are making a request for. Click Continue at the bottom of the form to make your appointment request.
  4. You should receive an email right away indicating that you have submitted your request. Within a day or two you should receive another email confirming your appointment.
  5. On your appointment day, bring your child to the Kloz 4 Kidz portable located behind Marysville United Methodist Church. At this time, we are requiring masks be worn by all family members. Please plan to arrive on time for your appointment. If you are not able to make your appointment please use the link provided in your confirmation email to cancel or reschedule.
  6. Give your name to the volunteer at the window beside the front porch and wait outside. The volunteer will bring your pre-bagged clothes out onto the porch, and will bring some shoes for your child to try on.
  7. Please have your child try on their shoes before you leave.
  8. Once your shoes have been selected, please exit the area, as others will be arriving for appointments or waiting for service.

At this time, we are not providing jeans or coats. Coats are provided during the winter months and will be available beginning November 1. Jeans will be available again once we are able to to provide fitting rooms.


Volunteering

We always need volunteers! We are an entirely volunteer-run organization; if you are considering coming to work with us, we are grateful. Without you we would not be able to continue serving Snohomish County families.

Current highest volunteer needs:

  • We are currently looking for a scheduling coordinator. This would be a great job for someone who prefers to help from home, on their own schedule. This person will be responsible for confirming client appointments and maintaining the volunteer schedule. We use online tools for some of the scheduling and tracking, so a level of comfort with technology would be a plus -- or a desire to learn!
  • Our greatest ongoing need is for people to work directly with families. These volunteers sign up for one or more 3 hour shifts per month, during which they help with clothing sizing and selection, and keep records of client visits. Training is provided, and a background check (provided by K4K) is required. Note that many of our dedicated, ongoing volunteers are in a high-risk age group for Covid-19, and to protect their health, it is absolutely essential that we get more help in this area.
  • As we prepare to reopen our portable for in-person shopping, we need volunteers to sort donations and stock our shelves. These volunteers check clothing for stains or tears, fold or hang them, and keep an eye on our inventory levels. A regular commitment is preferred, but even a single session helps, so this is a great way to pitch in if you don't have a lot of time to spare.
  • Because we launder all our clothing donations, we always need people to wash and dry loads of clothes at home. This is a super simple, low-commitment way to help. Volunteers simply take a bag of clothes home, wash, dry, and fold it, and return it to us.
  • Do you have idea about another way you could help? We want to hear your ideas! Let us know what you're thinking!

If you'd like more information about volunteering, have questions, or want to sign up, please email us at questions@kloz4kidz.com or k4kvolunteering@gmail.com.

How It Works

We offer free clothing on a seasonal basis to school-aged children residing in Snohomish County. Size availability varies depending upon donations, but in general we stock sizes from 4T up to teen/adult XXL.

A child may receive clothing from us every 4 months (maximum 3 times per year). At each visit, the child will receive a set of gently used, school-appropriate clothing, consisting of:

  • 3 shirts
  • 2 sweatshirts
  • 1-2 pairs of sweatpants and/or leggings (girls only)
  • 3 pairs of underwear (new)
  • 3 pairs of socks (new)
  • 1 pair of shoes

As a temporary health and safety measure during the current pandemic, we are not allowing shoppers inside our portable. Most clothing selections will be made by our volunteers using parent guidance provided on the appointment form.

At this time, since we are unable to provide fitting rooms, we are temporarily unable to provide jeans. We ask parents to have their children try on shoes outside the portable. Jeans will return to availability when we are able to safely open our building to shoppers.

To receive clothing for your child, please make an appointment using our online tool. As a part of the appointment process, you will be be asked questions about your child's age and clothing sizes and preferences. Get more information or begin your appointment process here.
